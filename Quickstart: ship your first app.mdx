# Quickstart: ship your first app

LaunchPulse helps you go from a rough idea to a working product without starting from a blank codebase. Describe what you want to build, choose the right app type, preview the result, improve it with prompts, and publish when the core experience is ready.

This guide walks you through your first LaunchPulse build.

<Info>
Best for first-time users who want to build a web app, mobile app, dashboard, game, workflow tool, SaaS MVP, or custom software product.
</Info>

## Before you start

A great first build starts with a clear product idea.

You do not need a full product spec, but you should know:

- who the app is for
- what problem it solves
- whether it should be a web app or mobile app
- the 3–5 most important features
- what the first successful version should do

<Tip>
Keep your first version focused. LaunchPulse works best when you start with one clear product flow, then improve it step by step.
</Tip>

## Step-by-step guide

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a new project">
    Open your LaunchPulse dashboard and start a new project. Give it a clear name so you can easily find it later.

    Good project names:
    - Fitness coach mobile app
    - Real estate agency dashboard
    - AI customer support portal
    - Restaurant booking workflow
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose Web App or Mobile App">
    Pick the app type that matches how users will experience the product.

    Choose **Web App** for dashboards, SaaS tools, portals, admin systems, and internal workflows.

    Choose **Mobile App** for app-store products, mobile-first experiences, games, and consumer apps.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Write your first prompt">
    Describe the app like you are briefing a product team.

    Include:
    - target users
    - main problem
    - must-have features
    - design direction
    - what version one should achieve
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review the first plan">
    LaunchPulse may structure your idea into pages, features, flows, and product logic. Review the plan before expanding the scope.

    Remove anything that is not needed for version one.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate the first version">
    Let LaunchPulse create the initial app experience. This may include screens, flows, UI, data structure, and app logic depending on the product.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Preview and test">
    Use the live preview to click through the app.

    Test the main flow first. For example:
    - Can a user sign up?
    - Can they complete the key action?
    - Does the dashboard make sense?
    - Does the mobile app feel good on a phone?
  </Step>

  <Step title="Improve with focused prompts">
    Ask for one meaningful improvement at a time.

    Examples:
    - “Improve the onboarding screen and make it feel more premium.”
    - “Add a client profile page with notes, status, and recent activity.”
    - “Fix the booking flow so users can choose a date before payment.”
  </Step>

  <Step title="Publish or prepare for launch">
    When the core experience works, prepare the app for launch.

    For web apps, publish and connect a custom domain when ready.

    For mobile apps, prepare for iOS and Android publishing.
  </Step>
</Steps>
