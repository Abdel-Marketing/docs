# App Screenshot Generator

LaunchPulse helps you create polished App Store and Play Store screenshots for your mobile app.

Instead of designing store screenshots from scratch, you can use your app screens, product references, and store listing goals to generate clean visuals that explain what your app does and why users should download it.

<Info>
App screenshots are one of the most important parts of a mobile app launch. They help users understand the product before they install it.
</Info>

## When to use the screenshot generator

Use the App Screenshot Generator when:

- your mobile app is close to launch
- you need App Store or Play Store screenshots
- you want to make your app listing look more professional
- you have real app screens but need better presentation
- you want to highlight key features clearly
- you are preparing marketing assets for a mobile MVP
- you want to improve how users understand the app before downloading

<Tip>
Generate screenshots after the core app flow is working. Screenshots are more useful when they show the real product experience.
</Tip>

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prepare your app screens">
    Use real screens from your LaunchPulse mobile app, such as onboarding, dashboard, profile, booking, checkout, game, or result screens.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add an App Store or Play Store link">
    If you already have a store listing or reference app, paste the link so LaunchPulse can understand the style and context.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Upload design references">
    Add reference screenshots, brand examples, or visual styles you want the screenshots to follow.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Describe the message">
    Tell LaunchPulse what each screenshot should communicate, such as “track workouts,” “book sessions,” “play quick battles,” or “manage clients.”
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate screenshot concepts">
    LaunchPulse helps create polished screenshot visuals that explain your app clearly.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review and refine">
    Ask for changes to copy, layout, background style, device framing, or feature emphasis.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What makes a good app screenshot

| Element | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Clear benefit | Users should understand the value in seconds |
| Real product screen | Screenshots should reflect the actual app experience |
| Short headline | Keep text readable and easy to scan |
| Clean layout | Avoid cluttered visuals or too many details |
| Strong first screenshot | The first image should communicate the main reason to download |
| Consistent style | Use a consistent visual system across all screenshots |
| Feature focus | Each screenshot should highlight one idea, not everything at once |

## Example screenshot prompt

```text
Create App Store screenshots for my fitness coach mobile app.
```

## Good screenshot checklist

Before generating screenshots, prepare:

- your app name
- the target audience
- 3–5 main benefits
- real app screens
- brand colors or style references
- App Store or Play Store requirements
- short headline ideas
- the main feature you want users to remember

<Warning>
Do not use screenshots that show broken states, test data, API errors, unfinished screens, or private user information.
</Warning>

## Common mistakes

| Mistake | Better approach |
|---|---|
| Showing too much in one screenshot | Highlight one benefit per image |
| Using tiny text | Keep headlines short and readable |
| Showing unfinished screens | Use polished screens from the real app |
| Making screenshots too generic | Explain what makes your app valuable |
| Ignoring store requirements | Prepare screenshots for the right platform and device sizes |
| Using private data | Use safe demo data only |
