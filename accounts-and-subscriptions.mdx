# Accounts & Subscriptions

LaunchPulse uses a subscription model built around **monthly AI credits**.

Your plan determines how many credits you receive each month, what product capabilities you can access, and whether you can deploy, download code, or use custom domains.

<Info>
LaunchPulse credits are used across core product experiences, including app building, screenshot generation, and AI app research workflows.
</Info>

## Plans at a glance

LaunchPulse currently offers three main plans:

|---|---:|---:|---|
| Free | $0 | 10,000 | Trying LaunchPulse, lightweight exploration, live preview |
| Starter | $19 | 200,000 | Individual builders shipping real apps |
| Builder | $49 | 500,000 | Heavier usage, advanced testing, more serious app building |

## What each plan includes

### Free

The Free plan is designed to help users try LaunchPulse and understand how the platform works.

**Included on Free:**

- 10,000 AI credits per month
- AI App Store screenshot generator access
- Live preview

**Best for:**

- exploring the product
- trying smaller builds
- testing the workflow before upgrading
- generating a limited number of screenshot or research outputs

<Warning>
The Free plan is for trying LaunchPulse. It does **not** include everything needed to fully ship and deploy a production app.
</Warning>

### Starter

The Starter plan is the main paid entry point for builders who want to move from experimentation to real shipping.

**Included on Starter:**

- 200,000 AI credits per month
- unlimited projects
- live preview and testing
- LaunchPulse Cloud
- publish to iOS App Store and Android Play Store
- one-click deployment for websites and web apps
- custom domains
- workspace / collaboration
- download code
- unlimited app store screenshot downloads

**Best for:**

- shipping websites and web apps
- building real SaaS products
- preparing mobile apps for release
- downloading and owning the generated code
- using LaunchPulse beyond basic experimentation

### Builder

The Builder plan is for users who need more capacity, stronger testing, and more advanced build workflows.

**Included on Builder:**

- everything in Starter
- 500,000 AI credits per month
- project sharing / stronger collaboration workflows
- autobuild
- advanced browser testing
- priority support

**Best for:**

- serious app builders
- heavier monthly usage
- faster iteration loops
- more demanding testing workflows
- teams or users who need more capacity and support

## What credits are used for

Credits power the AI-driven parts of LaunchPulse.

They are commonly used for:

- building and updating apps
- generating product flows and screens
- AI App Store screenshot generation
- AI app discovery / AI app research workflows
- mobile AI research outputs
- larger or repeated build iterations

<Tip>
If you are using LaunchPulse heavily for screenshot generation or AI app discovery, you may use credits faster than a light web-app user.
</Tip>

## What paid plans unlock

A paid subscription is important because it unlocks the parts of LaunchPulse used for actually shipping and owning the result.

| Capability | Free | Starter | Builder |
|---|---|---|---|
| Live preview | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| AI credits | 10,000/mo | 200,000/mo | 500,000/mo |
| AI App Store screenshots | Limited by credits | Yes | Yes |
| AI app discovery / research | Limited by credits | Yes | Yes |
| Unlimited projects | No | Yes | Yes |
| Live preview & testing | Limited | Yes | Yes |
| Advanced browser testing | No | No | Yes |
| Project sharing / collaboration | No | Yes | Yes |
| Download code | No | Yes | Yes |
| One-click deployment | No | Yes | Yes |
| Custom domains | No | Yes | Yes |
| Publish mobile apps | No | Yes | Yes |
| Priority support | No | No | Yes |

## Important subscription rule

Without a **paid subscription**, users cannot use the main shipping features.

<Warning>
If you do not have a paid subscription, you cannot:
- deploy your project
- download the code
- connect a custom domain
</Warning>

This means the Free plan is great for trying LaunchPulse, but real production shipping starts on a paid plan.

## Add-on credits

If a user runs out of monthly credits, LaunchPulse also offers **credit add-ons**.

These are useful for users who:

- need extra capacity before their monthly reset
- generate many screenshots
- do a lot of AI app discovery or research
- run heavier build sessions
- do not want to upgrade plans yet but need more credits right now

### Credit add-ons currently shown

| Add-on | Price |
|---|---:|
| 50K credits | $5 |
| 100K credits | $9 |
| 500K credits | $39 |
| 1M credits | $69 |

<Info>
Credit add-ons are one-time purchases designed to give users more room when they run out of monthly credits.
</Info>

## Monthly vs annual billing

If annual billing is enabled, users can save compared with paying month-to-month.

Based on the current pricing shown in the workspace:

| Plan | Monthly billing | Annual billing equivalent |
|---|---:|---:|
| Starter | $19/month | about $15/month billed annually |
| Builder | $49/month | about $39/month billed annually |

This makes annual billing a good fit for users who already know LaunchPulse is part of their normal workflow.

## Common account questions

### What happens if I run out of credits?

You can either:

- wait for your monthly credits to reset, or
- purchase add-on credits

If you use LaunchPulse often for screenshots, app research, or repeated iterations, add-ons can help you keep moving without interruption.

### Do screenshots use credits?

Yes. The screenshot workflow is credit-based.

### Does AI app discovery / mobile AI research use credits?

Yes. These workflows are also credit-based.

### Can I deploy on the Free plan?

No. Deployment requires a paid subscription.

### Can I download code on the Free plan?

No. Code download requires a paid subscription.

### Can I use a custom domain on the Free plan?

No. Custom domains require a paid subscription.

### Which plan should most users start with?

Most serious users should start with **Starter**. It is the main plan for people who want to move from experimenting to actually shipping.

## What makes a good subscriptions page

A strong subscriptions page should make it easy for users to understand:

- what they get on each plan
- how many credits they receive
- what those credits are used for
- which plan unlocks deployment and code access
- what to do when they run out of credits
- whether they should choose Free, Starter, or Builder
